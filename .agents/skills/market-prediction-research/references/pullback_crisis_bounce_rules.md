# Pullback, Crisis, Bounce, And Bottoming Rules

## Normal Pullback

Usually includes:

- equity decline
- VIX rise
- weaker breadth
- credit spreads not out of control
- banks not systemically collapsing
- funding markets functioning
- liquidity not frozen
- macro not rapidly deteriorating

## Crisis Warning

Usually includes:

- equity decline
- widening credit spreads
- bank stocks underperform sharply
- dollar funding pressure rises
- repo or money-market abnormality
- high-yield, leveraged-loan, or commercial real estate stress
- flight to cash, bills, gold, or dollar
- rising asset correlations
- market depth worsens and small selling moves price sharply
- policymakers send emergency signals

Rule: equities alone falling may be a pullback; equities, credit, funding, banks, and dollar liquidity worsening together looks like crisis risk.

## Dead-Cat Bounce

Typical features:

- short-covering driven
- insufficient volume
- weak breadth
- credit spreads still widening
- banks do not confirm
- VIX decline is temporary
- earnings expectations continue falling
- liquidity does not improve
- rebound fails at moving-average resistance

## More Reliable Bottom

Typical features:

- panic fully released
- credit spreads stop widening
- VIX spikes and reverses
- breadth repairs clearly
- equal-weight index outperforms
- small caps and cyclicals participate
- banks stabilize
- bad news no longer creates new lows
- policy or liquidity turns
- high volume followed by sustained absorption
- earnings downgrade speed slows
- money rotates from safe havens back to risk assets
